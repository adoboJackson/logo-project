Logo Contest

Paula Vazquezas designed by Emily Rush

Emily Rush: During my creative brief with Paula we discussed her favorite color (purple), favorite activities (crafting, sewing, spending time with her sister) and some important things going on in her life (she just bought a house!). I started off focused on Moroccan tile designs (one of her favorite interior decoration styles) and needle and thread graphics. I did a lot of hand sketches in that vein, but I wasn't really feeling any of the designs. Then I looked around the internet for inspiring fonts, and found one that had very ornate lettering that reminded me both of embroidery and birds nests. I re-drew and modified the "P", added the little birdie (which I guess represents Paula, weaving thread and nesting) and voilá! Award winning logo design.

Emily Rush as designed by Rob Gibson

Katie Rousos as designed by Gaby Fleming

Gaby Fleming: Katie said that she liked “Old Lady Stuff”, sewing, knitting, cats etc. I have learned that she enjoys teal, orange, and bold fonts. I like to experiment with type, so I found a cursive font, started tweaking it so much that now it looks nothing like the original. Her cat is grey, so I incorporated that into her last name. The K is supposed to be knitting needles, but I have been told they also look like scissors. Web Designer is done with stitching to show two of her creative outlets. The logo can also be done in a solid color, and without the web designer part for smaller applications.

Bryan Rosenbaum as designed by Jona Bustamante

Jona Bustamante: Bryan is a big sports and soccer fan so I knew I wanted to do something along the lines of sporty or collegiate. Bryan's design sensibility leans on clean and minimal, so I designed a basic shield with a monochromatic palette for simplicity and added some texture so it didn't look so flat. I thought a modern monogram would go well with it, so I sketched away with his initials (leaving behind my inclinations to curves and swashes). I finally rested on simple, minimal lines—and thus is the story behind Bryan's amazingly masculine and sporty logo to complement him!

Edie Granillo as designed by Neil Bernardo

Neil Bernardo: For Edie's logo, I integrated the shapes that form her initials (EG). Edie loves the color turquoise. I tried to incorporate elements of her personality and style so I went with an informal, iconic symbol that, I hope, suggests easygoing elegance. Here's an alternative version.

Gaby Fleming as designed by Paula Vazquez

Paula Vazquez: When I think of Gaby, I think of her trademark pink hair, happy personality and great creative work. After learning more about her likes and preferences, I found out that she also enjoys off-roading and dune buggies. This felt like a great juxtaposition to try and capture in her logo: an edgy hobby paired with her usual fun and girly personality. I used a flowing typeface with a lot movement for her name to keep the feminine aspect and then made up the tagline Pink Hair, Don't Care to help emphasize the unique contrast.

Neil Bernardo as designed by Katie Rousos

Katie Rousos: For Neil, he said that he liked geometric design as well as hand drawn design. I wanted to focus more on the geometric side as a challenge for myself because I don't normally design in that style. Neil also likes the color blue so I wanted to keep the color scheme in shades of blue. I added in a little bit of a 3D feel with the shading of the triangle planes to make it look like they were being pulled up in different spots.

Rob Gibson as designed by Edie Granillo

Edie Granillo: Rob mentioned in his questionnaire that he liked comic books and comic book art. That is something that piqued my interest from a design standpoint, since I've never explored that realm before. I wanted to encapsulate a logo that had a fantasy-superhero, masculine look. I had decided upon a 3 dimensional logo, working with planes for the 3 dimensionality, inspiration from Superman's Kryptonite helping long the way. I also incorporated in his favorite color purple (blue purple, not red purple).
